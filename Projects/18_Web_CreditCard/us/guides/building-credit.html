How to Build Credit From Scratch

With no credit history, you're often stuck in a catch-22: lenders want to see a track record, but you can't build one without an account. A few well-established paths work around this.

1. Start with an entry-level or secured card

See our student / first card roundup for cards designed for people with no prior credit history. Secured or FD-backed cards, where your credit limit is tied to a deposit, are widely available and are one of the most reliable approval routes.

2. Become an authorized or add-on user

If a family member adds you as an authorized or add-on user on their card, that account's history can help build your own profile over time, especially when the primary user has a long, on-time payment record.

3. Use the card lightly, and pay in full

You don't need to carry a balance to build credit. Paying your statement in full every cycle avoids interest while still reporting positive payment history. A good habit: put one or two small recurring charges on the card and set up autopay for the full balance.

4. Keep utilization low

Aim to use well under 30% of your available limit at any given time, since issuers typically report your balance at the end of the billing cycle.

5. Be patient and consistent

Meaningful score history takes months, with the biggest gains usually after 6–12 months of on-time payments. Avoid applying for several cards at once.
