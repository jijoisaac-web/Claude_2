Credit Score Basics in India: What Actually Moves the Number

In India, the most widely used credit score is the CIBIL Score, issued by TransUnion CIBIL, ranging from 300–900. Other bureaus — Experian, Equifax and CRIF High Mark — issue similar scores, and lenders may check any of them.

What moves your CIBIL Score

Payment history — On-time repayment of credit card bills and loan EMIs is the single biggest factor. Missed or late payments are reported and can significantly lower your score.

Credit utilization — Keeping your credit card balance well below your limit (commonly recommended under 30%) at statement time helps your score.

Credit history length — Older, well-managed accounts help. Closing your oldest card can shorten your average account age.

Credit mix — A mix of secured (like an FD-backed card or auto loan) and unsecured credit (credit cards, personal loans) is viewed favourably.

Hard inquiries — Applying for many cards or loans in a short period triggers multiple hard inquiries, which can temporarily lower your score.

Score ranges, roughly

Below 600 is considered poor and makes approval difficult; 600–749 is fair to good; 750 and above is generally considered good to excellent and qualifies for the best offers from most lenders.

Common myths

Checking your own CIBIL report is a "soft inquiry" and does not affect your score. You don't need to carry a revolving balance or pay interest to build a good score — paying your statement in full every month is the ideal habit.
