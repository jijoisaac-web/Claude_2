Credit Score Basics in Malaysia: What Actually Moves the Number

In Malaysia, lenders typically reference two related but distinct sources: your CCRIS report (Central Credit Reference Information System, maintained by Bank Negara Malaysia, showing your actual loan and credit card accounts and repayment conduct) and your CTOS Score (a commercial credit score from CTOS Data Systems, roughly on a 300–850 scale, that summarizes your credit risk).

What moves your score

Repayment conduct — CCRIS records whether your credit card and loan payments were made on time each month; this is the most heavily weighted factor in both CCRIS conduct and your CTOS Score.

Outstanding debt and utilization — How much you owe relative to your credit limits and income, including Bank Negara Malaysia's rule capping total credit limits for lower-income applicants.

Credit history length and mix — A longer track record across different credit types (credit cards, hire purchase, housing loans) generally supports a stronger score.

Recent applications — Applying for several cards or loans in a short window can signal higher risk to lenders reviewing your CCRIS report.

Regulatory guardrails

Bank Negara Malaysia requires a minimum annual income of RM24,000 for any first-time credit card applicant, and caps total credit limits at twice monthly income for cardholders earning RM36,000/year or less, who are also limited to cards from at most two banks.

Common myths

Checking your own CTOS report yourself does not lower your score. You don't need to carry a balance to build a good repayment record — paying your statement in full each month is reported as positive conduct in CCRIS.
