Disclaimer & Data Sources
Rates shown on this page are indicative estimates only, calculated using live mid-market exchange rates adjusted by each provider's typical margin and fee. They are not guaranteed and may differ from the actual rate offered at the time of your transfer.

Mid-market rate sources (fetched live, cross-checked across 3 APIs):
  • Frankfurter / European Central Bank (ECB) — api.frankfurter.app — Official ECB published rates. Most authoritative for EUR cross-rates.
  • fawazahmed0 Currency API — cdn.jsdelivr.net/npm/@fawazahmed0/currency-api — Free, open-source dataset covering 150+ currencies, updated daily.
  • currency-api.pages.dev — Cloudflare-hosted mirror of the above dataset, used as a redundant fallback.
These are interbank mid-market rates — the midpoint between buy and sell prices. Remittance providers add their own margin on top of this rate. The actual rate you receive will always be slightly lower than the mid-market rate shown.

Return-to-India Checklist
Track your preparation across 3 phases. Progress saves automatically.
Overall Progress 0%
1
6–12 Months Before Return
0 / 5 done
✓
Calculate your RNOR window
RNOR status shields foreign income from India tax for up to 2 years after return. Know your window before you book the flight.
✓
Start repatriating NRE FD maturity proceeds
NRE FD interest is fully tax-free while you're an NRI. Redeem maturing FDs and wire funds abroad — once you become Resident, NRE interest becomes taxable.
✓
Stop new NRO deposits — plan NRO repatriation
NRO repatriation is capped at USD 1M/year and requires CA certificate (Form 15CA/15CB). Start the process early — bank processing takes 2–4 weeks per transfer.
✓
Review overseas investments — sell tax-efficiently
Capital gains on overseas assets are not taxable in India during RNOR period. Review your portfolio abroad and consider selling appreciated positions before you lose RNOR status.
✓
Arrange health insurance in India
Your overseas employer insurance ends when you leave. Get a family floater health policy (₹10–20L cover) before arrival — pre-existing conditions may have a waiting period of 1–4 years.
2
1–3 Months Before Return
0 / 4 done
✓
Convert NRE account → Resident or RFC account
You must convert your NRE account within a reasonable time of becoming Resident. RFC (Resident Foreign Currency) account lets you hold foreign currency without converting to INR — useful if you earn abroad even after returning.
✓
Convert NRO account → Resident savings
NRO account should be re-designated as a regular resident savings account. Contact your bank's NRI desk — they'll require address proof and updated KYC documents.
✓
Update KYC with banks (new India address)
Failure to update KYC can freeze your accounts. Visit branch or use net banking portal to upload new address proof (Aadhaar/utility bill). Do this for every bank you hold accounts with.
✓
File Form 10F if DTAA claim is pending
If you have pending TDS refunds or active DTAA claims in India, file Form 10F with your Tax Residency Certificate before leaving your host country. This can be done online on the Indian Income Tax portal.
3
After Return — Within 6 Months
0 / 3 done
✓
Update Aadhaar with new India address
Visit an Aadhaar enrollment centre or use the UIDAI online portal with a valid address proof. Updated Aadhaar is required for bank KYC, mutual fund investments, and income tax filing.
✓
Notify all investments of resident status change
Mutual funds, demat account, and brokers must be notified. Your Demat account operating as NRI-PIS needs to be re-designated. Failure to notify may cause tax-withholding issues at redemption.
✓
File IT return — declare as RNOR for up to 2 years
File your Income Tax Return declaring RNOR status. Include Schedule FA to disclose foreign assets. Under RNOR, foreign income is still exempt — maximise this before your 2-year window closes.
📋 India Inflation Reality: CPI has averaged 5–7% p.a. over the last decade. Healthcare and education inflate faster (~8–10%). Kids education fees in metro schools: ₹10K–40K/month. Family health floater: ₹15K–40K/year. Build in a 20% buffer for unexpected expenses.

📋 What is Section 195 TDS?

When an NRI sells property in India, the buyer must deduct TDS on the entire sale consideration before paying. The buyer deposits this with the Indian government via Form 26QB. The NRI can claim refund if actual tax liability is lower.

💡 How to Reduce TDS?

Apply for Lower Deduction Certificate under Section 197 from your Assessing Officer before the sale. With capital gain exemptions (Section 54 — reinvest in another house within 2 years), your actual tax can be nil.

📄 Key Documents

Buyer needs: Form 15CA & 15CB (CA-certified). NRI needs: PAN card, TAN of buyer, sale agreement, cost basis proof. TDS certificate (Form 16B) issued to NRI within 15 days.

🌍 NRI & EPF Rules

NRIs can withdraw their full EPF after 2 months of leaving India (or becoming NRI). Submit Form 19 (EPF) and Form 10C (EPS) online via UAN portal. Processing takes 15–30 days.

💰 Taxability Rules

EPF withdrawal is fully tax-free if service ≥ 5 years. If < 5 years: taxable as salary income + 10% TDS if > ₹50,000. Exception: medical reasons, employer shutdown, or job transfer.

📊 EPS Pension Options

If service < 10 years: get lump sum (scheme certificate). If 10+ years: entitled to monthly pension at age 58. Maximum EPS contribution is ₹1,250/month (capped at ₹15,000 salary).

PAN & Aadhaar Guide for NRIs

Complete guide to getting, updating & using PAN and Aadhaar as an NRI — what's mandatory, what's optional

🪪 PAN Card — Is it Mandatory?

Yes for NRIs if you: own property in India, earn India income (rent, dividends, capital gains), invest in mutual funds or stocks, have NRE/NRO accounts, or file Indian income tax returns. Apply via Form 49AA (for foreign nationals / OCIs) or Form 49A (Indian citizens abroad). Processing takes 15–20 days. Cost: ₹107 (India address) or ₹1,017 (foreign address).

🔵 Aadhaar — NRI Rules

NRIs can apply for Aadhaar after residing in India for 182+ days in the preceding 12 months. It is NOT mandatory for NRIs to have Aadhaar. NRIs are exempt from compulsory PAN-Aadhaar linking. However, if you have both, linking avoids future TDS issues when you return.

🔗 Where PAN is Used
  • NRE / NRO account opening
  • Mutual fund investments
  • Property purchase / sale
  • TDS refund claims (ITR)
  • DTAA benefit applications
  • Demat & trading accounts
⚠️ Common NRI Mistakes
  • Using residential PAN after becoming NRI
  • Not updating PAN address to overseas
  • Linking Aadhaar without India address
  • Quoting wrong PAN in ITR (wrong status)
  • Not submitting Form 15CA/15CB on remittances
🔄 Updating Existing PAN

Update your address on PAN via NSDL / UTI portals online. Select 'Change in existing PAN data'. Submit proof of overseas address. Important: notify your bank & mutual fund about NRI status — different TDS rates apply.
