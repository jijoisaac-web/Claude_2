Indians in Singapore: Complete NRI Finance Guide 2025

Banking, tax treaties, SGD remittance, investments in India, and how to maximise your money as an Indian living in Singapore.

🇸🇬 Singapore Edition 📅 Updated July 2026 ⏱ 10 min read

Why Singapore Is Unique for NRIs

Singapore is home to over 600,000 Indians, making it one of the largest Indian diaspora communities in Asia. The combination of zero capital gains tax in Singapore, a strong SGD, and India's DTAA treaty creates exceptional wealth-building opportunities — if you know how to navigate both systems.

The SGD-INR exchange rate has been one of the most favourable for remittances, consistently ranging between ₹60–65 per SGD. With Singapore's median income for Indian professionals often exceeding SGD 7,000–10,000/month, the opportunity to build significant wealth in India is enormous.

Key stat: A Singapore-based NRI sending SGD 3,000/month to India over 10 years accumulates approximately ₹2.2 crore — before any investment returns on the Indian side.

NRI Status: Singapore Tax Residents

If you are a Singapore PR or Employment Pass holder spending more than 183 days outside India, you qualify as an NRI under Indian law. This changes your tax obligations significantly.

India-Singapore DTAA

India and Singapore signed a comprehensive Double Taxation Avoidance Agreement (DTAA). Key benefits for NRIs living in Singapore:

  • Salary income: Taxed only in Singapore (not India), as long as work is performed in Singapore
  • Dividends: Maximum withholding tax of 15% in India (reduced from standard 20%)
  • Interest on NRO accounts: Capped at 15% TDS (claim refund if your total income is below ₹5L)
  • Capital gains: Gains on Indian stocks held before a certain date may be protected — consult a CA
  • No double taxation: Singapore taxes paid can be credited against any Indian tax liability

Important: To claim DTAA benefits, submit Form 15G/15H or a Tax Residency Certificate (TRC) from Singapore's IRAS to your Indian bank. Without a TRC, banks deduct full TDS rates.

Remittance Strategy: Maximise What You Send Home

Timing Your Transfers

The SGD/INR rate moves with USD/INR. When the US dollar strengthens, your SGD typically buys more INR. Avoid sending money on Indian bank holidays or festival seasons (Diwali, Pongal) when demand spikes and spreads widen.

Regular vs Lump Sum

For most Singapore NRIs, a monthly standing order into an NRE Fixed Deposit works best. You benefit from rupee cost averaging on the exchange rate and lock in competitive FD rates (currently 6.5–7.5% for NRE FDs at major Indian banks).

Transfer Amount Thresholds

  • Under SGD 1,000: Use Wise or InstaReM for best rates
  • SGD 1,000–10,000: Compare bank wire vs Wise; difference is often SGD 30–80
  • Above SGD 10,000: Negotiate rates directly with your Singapore bank's forex desk

Investing in India from Singapore

NRE Fixed Deposits — Still the Safest Bet

NRE FDs offer tax-free interest in India at 6.5–7.5% p.a. For a Singapore resident paying 0% Singapore income tax on foreign-sourced income, this is a genuinely tax-free return. Compare this with Singapore Savings Bonds at 2.5–3% — the NRE FD differential is substantial.

Mutual Funds: Can Singapore NRIs Invest?

Yes, but with conditions. Most Indian AMCs accept NRI investments from Singapore. However, you need a PAN card and must complete NRI KYC with a SEBI-registered intermediary. FATCA compliance forms (self-declaration) are required.

  • Allowed without issues: Most large AMCs (SBI, HDFC, ICICI Prudential, Mirae)
  • Some restrictions: A few fund houses have restrictions for Singapore residents due to local securities laws
  • Tax on gains: Equity LTCG over ₹1.25L taxed at 12.5%; STCG at 20%

NPS (National Pension System) for Singapore NRIs

NRIs can contribute to NPS Tier-1 accounts and claim a deduction under Section 80CCD(1B) up to ₹50,000 — in addition to the ₹1.5L under Section 80C. However, for NRIs with minimal Indian income, these deductions may have limited value. The bigger benefit is long-term retirement corpus building at regulated low-cost pension fund rates.

Indian Stocks (Direct Equity)

Singapore NRIs can invest via the Portfolio Investment Scheme (PIS) route through a designated PIS bank account linked to a demat account. Gains are taxable in India; ensure you report them in your Singapore income tax return as well (foreign-sourced income exemption may apply depending on when the gains arise).

CPF vs Indian Retirement Planning

Singapore's CPF (Central Provident Fund) is mandatory for Singapore PRs and citizens. If you are on an Employment Pass (EP), you do not contribute to CPF. Understanding this split is critical:

Factor Singapore CPF India NPS / Mutual Funds
Returns 2.5–4% (guaranteed) 8–12% (market-linked)
Currency risk SGD (stable) INR (inflation ~5–6%)
Withdrawal Age 55 onwards (phased) 60% lump sum at 60; 40% annuity
Portability Withdrawn on leaving Singapore Stays in India permanently
Tax Tax-free in Singapore Partial tax-exempt in India

Strategy: If you plan to retire in India, maximise NRE FD + Indian equity mutual funds. If you plan to retire in Singapore, maximise CPF SA (Special Account) contributions via voluntary top-ups for the 4% guaranteed return.

Tax Filing: What Singapore NRIs Must Do

Indian Tax Return (ITR)

You must file an ITR in India if any of the following apply:

  • Your Indian income (rent, FD interest, capital gains) exceeds ₹2.5L in a financial year
  • You have capital gains from selling Indian property or stocks
  • You want to claim a TDS refund on NRO account interest
  • You hold Indian assets and wish to repatriate funds

Use ITR-2 if you have capital gains or foreign income, otherwise ITR-1 suffices. File by 31 July of the assessment year (or 31 October with audit).

Singapore Tax Return

Singapore taxes income earned in Singapore. Foreign-sourced income (NRE interest, Indian rental income remitted to Singapore) is generally exempt from Singapore tax if it meets certain conditions. Consult an IRAS-registered tax advisor for your specific situation.

KrisFlyer Miles for India Flights

Singapore Airlines' KrisFlyer is one of the best frequent flyer programs for India routes. Key redemption sweet spots for Singapore NRIs:

  • Singapore → Mumbai/Chennai/Delhi (Economy): ~17,500 KrisFlyer miles one-way
  • Singapore → Mumbai/Delhi (Business): ~41,750 miles one-way (Saver award)
  • Peak periods to avoid: Diwali (Oct–Nov), Christmas, Pongal (Jan) — award space is very limited
  • Best time to book: 330 days before travel for Saver awards; watch for spontaneous partner releases

Tip: SIA's KrisFlyer Spree shopping portal and DBS/OCBC credit card partnerships allow you to earn miles on everyday spending in Singapore. An NRI spending SGD 5,000/month on a miles card can earn 60,000–80,000 miles per year — enough for a Business Class redemption.

Property Purchase in India from Singapore

NRIs in Singapore can purchase residential and commercial property in India freely. Agricultural land and plantation property require RBI permission. Key points:

  • Payment must come from NRE/NRO account or through banking channels — not cash
  • TDS on rent: If you earn rent from Indian property, tenant deducts 30% TDS (reduced to 20% with DTAA form)
  • TDS on sale: Buyer deducts 20–30% TDS on sale proceeds; you claim excess as refund while filing ITR
  • FEMA: Ensure all property transactions comply with FEMA regulations to avoid issues when repatriating sale proceeds

Essential Checklist for Singapore NRIs

  • ✅ Open NRE + NRO accounts with a major Indian bank
  • ✅ Get your PAN card — mandatory for all Indian financial transactions
  • ✅ Apply for Tax Residency Certificate (TRC) from Singapore IRAS annually
  • ✅ Submit TRC + Form 15G/15H to your Indian bank to reduce TDS
  • ✅ Complete FATCA self-declaration for mutual fund investments
  • ✅ Set up monthly auto-transfer to NRE FD for savings
  • ✅ Nominate a trusted person as Power of Attorney for Indian property matters
  • ✅ File ITR in India if Indian income exceeds ₹2.5L
  • ✅ Keep Singapore tax returns and payslips for DTAA claims
  • ✅ Review NRE/NRO account status if planning to return to India — convert to resident accounts within set timelines
