Options momentum scanner · who has flow behind them

Scans ~30 liquid F&O names and scores the bullishness of their options flow: PCR, fresh put-vs-call writing today, put-wall support proximity, room to the call wall, position vs max pain, and call unwinding overhead. High score = option writers are positioned for upside — momentum-long friendly. Stops/targets come from the OI walls themselves.
OI reads are strongest near expiry and for liquid names. Writers (sellers) drive OI walls: heavy put OI = a level someone is paid to defend; heavy call OI = a ceiling someone is paid to cap. Data ~3-min delayed during market hours; stale after close. Not investment advice.

IV vs realized volatility · who's priced rich vs cheap

Scans the same ~30 liquid F&O names and compares each one's live ATM implied volatility (what the option chain is currently pricing) against its own realized volatility (how much it has actually moved, annualized from the last 20/60 trading days). IV running well above realized = the market is pricing in more movement than has actually been happening — historically the more favorable side to be selling premium (credit spreads, strangles). IV well below realized = premium looks cheap relative to actual movement — more favorable for buying it (long straddle/strangle). This is a relative read, not a timing signal, and says nothing about direction.
IV is the chain's live ATM figure (avg of call/put where both exist); realized vol uses close-to-close log returns, no overnight-gap or intraday adjustment. Neither is forecasting the other — a rich reading can stay rich (or get richer) around known events (earnings, budget, RBI policy). Not investment advice.

Weekly maintenance

Rules that save you

1. Confluence over quantity. Trade the few names where the technical score (70+), options flow (LONG FLOW) and market gate all agree — not every idea on the list. A+ setups appear a few times a week, not a few times a day.
2. The stop is the decision. Size every position so the stop costs ≤1% of capital, and honor it mechanically. A stopped-out trade never becomes an "investment" — that's how traders become bagholders.
3. Decide the game before entry. The decision brief tells you: TRADING ONLY names are rented, not owned. INVESTABLE names may be accumulated. Never let one morph into the other mid-trade.
4. Distrust your own backtests. A combination you tuned until it looked great is curve-fit. Trust rules that work across universes and periods — and kill signals your own data shows don't pay (your smallcap test: gap-ups earned their keep, RSI-oversold didn't).
5. When the gate is red, do less. Weak breadth + institutions selling = smaller size or cash. Missing a rally costs nothing; catching a falling market costs compounding.

Options spread probability — indices & F&O stocks

NSE doesn't publish free historical option premiums, so this isn't a replay of real option prices. Instead it measures how far the underlying actually moved between past expiry-length windows and turns that into a historical "closed inside the range" hit rate for a spread width you pick — since NSE options are cash-settled on the closing level, that maps directly to probability of profit for credit spreads/iron condors (ignoring premium collected, brokerage, and slippage). Weekly/monthly windows are trading-day proxies (5d / 21d), not exact calendar expiry days, because NSE has changed the actual expiry weekday more than once (NIFTY weekly moved Thu→Tue in Sep 2025; BankNifty weekly was discontinued in Nov 2024 — it's monthly-only now; shown here in weekly form too, for comparison only). Stock options in India don't have weekly expiries at all, so weekly is disabled for the ~30 liquid F&O names below — monthly only. Below the historical tables, a live risk-profile panel pulls the real NSE option chain to show actual ₹ max profit/loss and breakeven for 1 lot of each strategy.
